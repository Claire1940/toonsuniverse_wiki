<Callout type="warning" title="Shield Timing">

<Section icon={Activity} title="Advanced Mechanics: The Shield Meta">

Mastering the Guard ability is the difference between a good Copper player and a legendary one. The shield doesn't just block a hit; it provides a window of immunity that can be used to bypass environmental hazards.

| Enemy Type | Shield Strategy | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **G&R** | Activate before entering the detection radius. | Complete detection negation. |
| **Typo** | Trigger during the skill check failure animation. | Prevents the instant damage tick. |
| **Squittle** | Use when a teammate is cornered in a room. | Allows the teammate to run through the enemy. |
| **Koi** | Shield yourself while kiting. | Negates the slow-down effect of Koi's proximity. |

One overlooked aspect of Copper's gameplay is her **Sanity (Sandy)** management. Her sanity drains slower than the average agent, meaning she can stay in "dark" zones or near enemies longer without suffering the debilitating effects of low sanity. This makes her the ideal candidate for staying behind to finish a machine while the rest of the team retreats to safety.

<Callout type="tip" title="Self-Shielding">
While Copper is primarily a support, her ability description states she can shield herself. To do this, ensure you are not targeting an ally when you press the ability button. This is vital for solo-clutching floor 5.
</Callout>
