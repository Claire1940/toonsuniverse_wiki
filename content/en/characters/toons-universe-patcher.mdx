## Understanding Patcher’s Abilities

Patcher's kit revolves around two core mechanics: item discovery and direct health restoration. Understanding the limitations of these skills is vital to preventing team wipes.

### Passive Ability: Rescue
At the beginning of every floor, Patcher’s "Rescue" passive triggers. This ability highlights all healing items (Bandages and Medkits) on the map for a short duration. 

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Use the first few seconds of a floor to call out item locations to your team. Since the highlight is temporary, memorizing the general direction of a Medkit can save a teammate’s life three minutes later.

### Active Ability: Patchup
Patchup is the most reliable healing mechanic in the game. It allows Patcher to restore exactly one heart of health to a selected agent. However, this ability comes with a high-risk trade-off: **both the healer and the recipient must remain completely stationary** during the process.

| Patchup Mechanic | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Max Charges** | 2 |
| **Recharge Method** | Completing machines (Malachor or PC). |
| **Healing Amount** | 1 Heart. |
| **Movement Penalty** | Both players are rooted until the heal completes. |

The Bear Emblem provides a free healing item every five floors, provided you have an empty inventory slot. This effectively doubles Patcher's utility by giving him physical items to hand out while he saves his Patchup charges for emergencies.

| Item Drop | Chance | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Bandage** | 75% | Quick patch for non-critical teammates. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not let your inventory stay full! If you hit a five-floor milestone with a full inventory, you will lose the Bear Emblem’s free item proc for that cycle.

## Advanced Strategies for 2026

To truly excel as a **toons universe patcher**, you must adopt a "objective-first" mindset. Since your heals are tied to machine completion, being "greedy" for machines is actually a selfless act. If your teammates are hogging the PCs, they are inadvertently preventing you from being able to save them later.

### 1. The "Machine Priority" Loop
Always aim to have at least one charge of Patchup stored. If you have two charges, you should look for a wounded teammate immediately. Completing a machine while you already have two charges is "wasted" recharge potential. Communicate with your team: "Let me finish this PC so I can get a heal charge."

### 2. Healing Under Pressure
Because Patchup roots you in place, never attempt a heal in open corridors where an Infected might path. Instead, lure your teammate into a side room or behind large furniture. The animation takes a few seconds, which is more than enough time for a Copper or GNR to close the distance.

### 3. Synergizing with Other Delvers
Patcher works exceptionally well with "Distractor" type characters. While a teammate like Fizzle keeps the Infected busy, Patcher can safely use his Patchup ability on wounded allies in the "safe zone." If you can obtain the **Selena’s Emblem**, your objective speed increases by 15%, allowing you to cycle through heal charges significantly faster.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Patcher get a heal charge from every objective?**

A: Patcher earns a chance at a heal charge upon the completion of any major machine, including Malachor extraction machines and PCs. However, the charge is only granted if you have contributed a significant amount of progress to that specific machine.

**Q: Can Patcher heal himself using the Patchup ability?**

A: No. The Patchup active ability is specifically designed to restore health to *other* agents. To heal himself, Patcher must rely on the Bandages and Medkits he finds using his "Rescue" passive or items generated by his Bear Emblem.

**Q: What happens if an Infected attacks during the Patchup animation?**

A: The animation will be interrupted if either player takes damage or if the Patcher player forced-moves to dodge. This will waste the time spent and potentially result in both players taking a hit, so always ensure the area is clear before starting a **toons universe patcher** heal.

**Q: Is the Bear Emblem better than other stat-boosting emblems?**

A: For a dedicated healer, yes. While speed or hacking emblems are useful, the ability to generate a Medkit (25% chance) every five floors is a massive advantage in deep delves where resources become extremely scarce.
