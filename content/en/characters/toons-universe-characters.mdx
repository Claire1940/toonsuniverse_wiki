## The Character Philosophy: No More "Mains"

One of the most significant departures in this game is the removal of the "Main Character" hierarchy. In similar titles, players often feel forced to grind for a specific "Main" character because they are objectively better than the rest of the cast. In Toons Universe, the developers have intentionally balanced the roster so that every agent has a distinct purpose.

All **toons universe characters** are unlocked through specific gameplay challenges rather than luck-based gacha systems or research capsules. This means that a starter character like Nimbus can remain just as relevant on Floor 30 as a late-game unlock.

| Feature | Toons Universe System | Traditional "Dandy-like" System |
|---|---|---|
| **Hierarchy** | Flat (No "Mains" vs "Non-Mains") | Vertical (Mains are always superior) |
| **Unlocking** | **Gameplay Challenges** | Research Capsules / RNG |
| **Viability** | All characters are viable | Starters often become obsolete |
| **Stat Balance** | Niche specializations | Linear power creep |

## Starter Characters: Nimbus vs. Waffle

When you begin your journey, you aren't forced to choose between a "boy" or "girl" starter and lock the other away. You start with both Nimbus and Waffle unlocked. However, choosing which one to play first depends heavily on whether you are playing alone or with a group.

### Nimbus: The Solo Specialist
Nimbus is often considered the superior choice for solo players. His primary ability allows him to restore his stamina to full on the spot. Because the sanity system makes stamina a precious resource, being able to refill your bar instantly is a life-saver during long chases. The trade-off is a brief window of vulnerability where he cannot move, requiring careful timing.

### Waffle: The Co-op Support
Waffle shines in a team environment. Her main ability involves throwing waffles at teammates, which automatically enter their inventory and provide a speed boost. Unlike other support characters in the genre, the recipient doesn't have to stop to pick the item up. While she lacks a self-buff for solo play, her utility in a group makes her an essential "toons universe character" for high-level team runs.

| Character | Primary Role | Best For | Key Ability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nimbus** | Survival / Stamina | **Solo Play** | Instant Stamina Recharge |
| **Waffle** | Support / Speed | **Co-op Play** | Waffle Toss (Speed Buff) |

> **💡 Tip:** If you're just learning the maps, stick with Nimbus. The ability to recover stamina will forgive many early-game movement mistakes.

## Understanding Sanity and Stealth

To play **toons universe characters** effectively, you must understand that Sanity is your Stealth. In this game, the "Infected" (monsters) are more attracted to players with high Sanity.

*   **High Sanity:** You are very visible. The Infected will prioritize chasing you.
*   **Low Sanity:** You are "stealthy." The Infected are less likely to notice you or may switch targets away from you.

This creates a unique dynamic where being chased actually makes you harder to find later, but at the cost of your stamina regeneration. If your Sanity hits zero, your stamina will drain completely and won't recover until the chase ends.

### The Distractor Role
Characters like **Fantasia** and **Fizzle** are specifically designed as "Distractors." They have the base speed required to loop the Infected. However, even the fastest **toons universe characters** can't outrun specific monsters like Teepo or Copper indefinitely. Distracting is an intentional mechanic here, meant for players who want to keep the monster occupied while others finish machines.

## How to Unlock New Toons Universe Characters

Unlocking new agents is a matter of dedication rather than luck. Since there are no research capsules to find, you progress by completing specific in-game milestones. Some of these are tied to "Event Floors" which occur randomly.

| Character | Unlock Requirement (Examples) | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| **Teepo** | Complete specific machine hacks / RNG Events | Medium (RNG Dependent) |
| **Fantasia** | Survive a certain number of chases | Easy |
| **Fizzle** | Interact with a set number of Fuse Boxes | Medium |
| **Advanced Agents** | Complete Gas Floor or Blackout objectives | Hard |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not "babysit" players on machines if you are being chased. Because players on machines usually have high Sanity, the Infected will often switch targets to them if you run too close, effectively "griefing" your teammate.

## Machine Types and Agent Stats

Every floor requires the completion of machines to power the elevator. There are two primary interaction types that correspond to the stats of your **toons universe characters**:

1.  **Hacking:** Tied to the "Hack" stat. These machines require logic-based interactions.
2.  **Extraction:** Tied to the "Extraction" stat. These are usually physical or mechanical tasks.
3.  **Tower Machines:** Once the main machines are done, a central tower activates, requiring a mix of both stats to finish the floor.

When building a team, ensure you have a balance of high-hack and high-extraction agents. If everyone plays a high-speed distractor, the machines will take far too long to complete, increasing the risk of a total party wipe.

## Essential Team Strategies for 2026

Success in the later floors of Toons Universe depends on resource management. Items are found on the floor, but the most reliable source is **Checkov’s Shop** located in the elevator.

*   **Limited Supply:** Checkov only carries a few of each item (often fewer than there are players).
*   **Sharing is Caring:** If you have full health, do not buy the medkit. Let the injured players take priority.
*   **Item Buffs:** Some agents receive passive buffs when holding or using items, making them the designated "mules" for the team.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best character for beginners in Toons Universe?**

A: **Nimbus** is the best starter character for beginners. His ability to instantly refill his stamina bar allows new players to survive longer chases while they learn the layout of the floors and machine locations.

**Q: How do I unlock characters without research capsules?**

A: Unlike other games in the genre, **toons universe characters** are unlocked by completing specific gameplay challenges, such as fixing a certain number of breakers during blackouts or surviving gas leak floors. You can check your progress in the character select menu.

**Q: Why does the monster stop chasing me and kill my teammate on a machine?**

A: This is due to the **Sanity system**. Sanity acts as your "stealth" stat. Players who have been working on machines usually have higher Sanity, making them more "attractive" to the Infected. If you lead a monster too close to a teammate, the monster will likely switch targets to the player with higher Sanity.

**Q: Are there any "Main" characters that are stronger than others?**

A: No. The developers have stated there is no character hierarchy. While some characters are better at specific tasks (like Fantasia for distracting or Waffle for support), every character is designed to be viable even in high-floor gameplay.
