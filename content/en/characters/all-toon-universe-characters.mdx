<Section icon={Shield} title="Understanding Sanity and Stealth">

In Toons Universe, sanity is not just a health bar—it is your stealth. This creates a unique risk-reward loop where being "healthy" actually makes you a bigger target for the infected.

**How the Sanity System Works:**
1. **The Stealth Paradox**: Infected prefer players with **higher sanity**. If you are at full sanity, you are the highest priority target in the room.
2. **The Chase Penalty**: When an infected chases you, your sanity drops. Once it hits zero, your stamina will stop regenerating until the chase ends and sanity is recovered.
3. **Machine Griefing**: Running past a player on a machine while being chased is dangerous. Because the player on the machine has higher sanity (stealth), the infected will likely switch targets to them immediately.

| Sanity Level | Stealth Effect | Stamina Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High (80-100%)** | Low Stealth (High Visibility) | Normal Regeneration |
| **Medium (40-79%)** | Moderate Stealth | Normal Regeneration |
| **Low (1-39%)** | High Stealth (Low Visibility) | Normal Regeneration |
| **Zero (0%)** | Maximum Stealth | **Regeneration Disabled** |

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Griefing Rule">
Never stand next to a player working on a machine if you are being chased. Your lower sanity might make you "invisible" to the monster, causing it to attack the innocent player instead.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Distractor Class: Fantasia and Fizzle">

Distracting is an intentional gameplay mechanic in Toons Universe. While any character can loop an infected for a short time, true distractors like **Fantasia and Fizzle** are built for prolonged engagement.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Infected">
    Recognize if the infected is a "speeder" like Teepo or Copper. These monsters are faster than all agents, so even distractors must use environmental obstacles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Monitor Sanity">
    As a distractor, your sanity will hit zero quickly. You must time your stamina usage perfectly, as it will not recover until you break the line of sight.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Lead Away from Machines">
    Always loop the infected in dead zones. If you bring a monster near a high-sanity teammate, the monster will "snap" to them, ending your distraction and likely killing your teammate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Distractor Stats">
Distractors usually have lower hack and extraction stats but significantly higher base speed and stamina pools to compensate for the lack of regeneration during chases.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Zap} title="Event Floors and Machine Types">

Survival isn't just about the monsters; it's about the environment. Toons Universe features several event floors that can drastically change your strategy for **all toon universe characters**.

**Common Event Types:**
- **Gas Floors**: These floors cause constant stamina drain. You must prioritize speed over stealth to finish the machines before your team is exhausted.
- **Blackouts**: All lights fail. You must find the breaker box. **Pro Tip**: Look for white particle effects on breaker boxes before the lights go out to predict a blackout.
- **Elevator Breakdowns**: Occurs after the final machine. You must find fuses scattered across the map to power the exit.
