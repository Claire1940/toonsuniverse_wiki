## Understanding Emblem Mechanics

Emblems are specialized pieces of equipment that agents can equip to gain passive advantages. Currently, each agent can equip up to two emblems simultaneously. Unlike basic items found in the shop, emblems provide permanent buffs that remain active throughout the duration of a run.

The power level of these items is intentionally high. Developers designed this system to provide a sense of progression; while the game remains challenging for new players, those who invest time into unlocking the full roster of **toons universe emblems** will find themselves significantly more capable.

### How to Unlock Emblems

Unlocking emblems requires significant time investment in specific agents. To obtain an agent's unique emblem, you must reach **Level 100** with that character. While this sounds daunting, the XP system is generous for active players.

| Action | XP Reward Potential |

## S-Tier Analysis: The Meta-Definers

### Tibo's Tadpole Emblem
The Tadpole is arguably the strongest emblem in the game due to its stacking potential. A single player provides a 15% boost to the entire team's maximum stamina. In a full lobby of 10 players, if everyone equips the Tadpole, the team receives a staggering **150% stamina boost**. This transforms even the slowest agents into marathon runners, making it nearly impossible for infected to catch players during long rotations.

### Selina's Bat Emblem
While Selina is a late-game unlock, her Bat emblem is essential for high-speed runs. It provides a 15% speed boost to all "objectives," which includes both computers (hacking) and extractors (levers). This versatility makes it superior to specialized emblems like the Squid, which only affects one type of machine.

## A-Tier Analysis: Efficiency and Utility

### Nimbus' Sheep Emblem
The Sheep emblem is a powerhouse for distractors. By reducing stamina consumption by 30%, it effectively extends your sprint time without needing to wait for a full recharge. This is particularly effective on agents who already have high base stamina or speed-boosting passives.

### Guido & Rossom's Snake Emblem
Knowledge is power in Toons Universe. The Snake emblem highlights machines through walls every minute. For players who haven't yet memorized every possible spawn location on every floor, this emblem is a game-changer. It reduces the "downtime" spent wandering the halls, leading to faster floor completions and less risk of accidental encounters with the infected.

## B-Tier Analysis: Survivability and Niche Power

### Patcher's Bear Emblem
The Bear emblem provides a safety net for teams struggling with health management. Every five floors, you receive a healing item (75% chance for Band-aids, 25% for a Medkit). 

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Bear emblem only triggers if you have an empty inventory slot. Always ensure you have space before transitioning to a 5th, 10th, or 15th floor to avoid wasting the proc.

### Koi's Fish Emblem
This emblem is specifically designed for agents with long, impactful cooldowns. By cutting 50% of your current cooldown upon finishing an objective, it allows agents like Koi to use their "Dive" ability much more frequently. It is a "safe" pick for players who rely heavily on their active abilities to escape tight situations.

## Strategy: Which Emblems to Unlock First?

For new players entering the 2026 season, the order in which you unlock **toons universe emblems** will dictate your progression speed. We recommend focusing on agents that provide team-wide benefits or high-utility passives early on.

1.  **Tibo (Tadpole):** Since Tibo is available early, getting him to Level 100 should be your first priority. The stamina boost helps you and every team you join.
2.  **Guido & Rossom (Snake):** Unlocking this early will help you learn the map layouts and machine spawns, which is a skill that carries over even after you switch to other emblems.
3.  **Patcher (Bear):** If you find yourself running out of health frequently, Patcher's emblem provides the sustain needed to reach deeper floors where XP gains are higher.
4.  **Selina (Bat):** Once you have the Malicor to unlock Selina, switch your focus to her. The objective speed is the ultimate "end-game" stat for efficient farming.

## FAQ

**Q: Do emblems stay unlocked if I prestige an agent?**

A: Yes, once you reach Level 100 and unlock the emblem, it is permanently added to your collection and can be used by any agent on your roster.

**Q: Can I equip two of the same emblem?**

A: No, you must equip two different **toons universe emblems**. However, the effects of certain emblems (like the Tadpole) stack if *different* players in the lobby have them equipped.

**Q: Is the Dragon Emblem from Copper worth the grind?**

A: The Dragon Emblem provides a shield for the first minute of each floor. While strong, it is highly situational as most damage is taken later in the floor when the infected have more pressure. It is currently considered an "Unknown" tier until more testing is done in high-level play.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get an agent to Level 100?**

A: Focus on "Full Clears." Completing every objective on a floor provides a massive XP bonus compared to just rushing the exit. Additionally, playing with a full team increases the total number of objectives, providing more opportunities for XP gain.
