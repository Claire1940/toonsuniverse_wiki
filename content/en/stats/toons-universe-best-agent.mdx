To unlock Koi, players must typically invest a significant amount of Melacore—roughly 5,000 units—making her one of the more expensive acquisitions in the game. ## Deep Dive into Abilities

The reason many label Koi as the **toons universe best agent** lies in her ability to manipulate the game's cooldown and hit-detection systems. Her kit is designed to reward players who stay focused on the objective while under pressure.

### Active Ability: Dive
The **Dive** ability allows Koi to submerge into the ground, granting her total invincibility. This state lasts as long as the player has stamina, but it comes with a significant 75-second cooldown.

*   **Tactical Use:** Dive is best used when cornered by lethal threats like Selena or Patcher. You can sprint while diving to reposition faster, though this drains your stamina bar rapidly.
*   **The Downside:** You cannot cancel the ability early; you must remain submerged until the stamina is depleted or the duration ends. This requires the player to reach a safe zone before surfacing, as you will be left with zero stamina and vulnerable to immediate attack.

### Passive Ability: Cheer
Koi’s passive, **Cheer**, is a game-changer for team synergy. Whenever an objective (such as a hacking machine) is completed, the nearest agent’s cooldowns are instantly reset.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Coordinate with your teammates. If an agent with a powerful long-cooldown ability is nearby, let Koi finish the final 1% of a machine to "Cheer" them, allowing for back-to-back ability usage.

## Survival Strategies for High-Floor Runs

Reaching floor 32 and beyond requires more than just good luck; it requires mastery of the environment and enemy patterns. When playing as the **toons universe best agent**, follow these survival protocols:

1.  **Stamina Management:** Never let your stamina drop to zero unless you are in the Dive state. Always keep a small reserve for emergency sprinting.
2.  **Infected Baiting:** Koi can take hits for the team. If a teammate is about to be downed by Selena, use Dive to intercept the path of the Infected. You will take no damage, and your teammate can escape.
3.  **Hacking Efficiency:** Despite her bulky "flap" hands, Koi is surprisingly efficient at hacking. Focus on the machines nearest to the "Main" area to ensure a quick exit once the fuses are collected.
4.  **Item Prioritization:** Always carry at least one bandage. While Koi has high survival, her bald-headed design doesn't protect her from accidental hits when Dive is on its 75-second cooldown.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The elevator in the current build can be buggy. Ensure all teammates are inside and the "Get to Elevator" command is used to prevent players from being left behind on higher floors.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Koi really the toons universe best agent for solo players?**

A: Yes. Her Dive ability provides a "get out of jail free" card that is essential when you don't have teammates to distract Infecteds. Her ability to reset her own cooldowns (if she is the "nearest agent" to a completed objective) allows for high uptime on her invincibility.

**Q: How do I manage the long cooldown on Dive?**

A: The 75-second cooldown is Koi's biggest weakness. You should only use Dive when an Infected is actively chasing you or when you need to cross a dangerous area with Squirtle tentacles. Between Dives, play defensively and stay near cover.

**Q: What is the best way to farm Melacore for Koi?**

A: Aim for high-floor runs. Reaching floor 30+ can net you upwards of 5,000 Melacore in a single session. Utilizing the "Job Well Done" buff (currently more effective than "Motivator" in the 2026 early access build) will significantly increase your earnings.

**Q: Can Koi survive the G and R boss fight?**

A: Absolutely. By timing your Dive during the boss's most aggressive phases, you can achieve complete immunity. This makes Koi the safest pick for players who struggle with the mechanical demands of the Guido and Rusem encounters.
