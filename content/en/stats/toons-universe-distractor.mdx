## The Sanity Mechanic: Toons Universe Stealth Explained

At the heart of the **toons universe distractor** role is the sanity bar, located prominently on the left side of your HUD. Unlike traditional horror games where proximity or line-of-sight are the only factors for aggression, Toons Universe utilizes sanity as a "stealth" value. The infected in this game are inherently attracted to "fresher" minds—meaning players with higher sanity levels.

When an infected begins a chase, your sanity begins to deplete. As this bar drops, you actually become "quieter" to the infected's AI. This leads to a counter-intuitive situation: the longer you successfully distract an enemy, the more likely they are to lose interest in you and lunge toward a nearby teammate who is peacefully working on a machine. This makes positioning critical for anyone attempting to be a **toons universe distractor**.

| Sanity Level | Effect on Agro | Stamina Regen Status | Stealth Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **75% - 100%** | Primary Target | Standard Regen | Low (Very Visible) |
| **25% - 74%** | Secondary Target | Capped Regen | Medium |
| **1% - 24%** | Ignored by AI | Heavily Capped | High |
| **0%** | Non-Target | **No Regeneration** | Invisible (Until Bumped) |

> **⚠️ Warning:** If your sanity hits zero, your stamina will stop regenerating entirely until the chase ends and you find a way to restore your mind. Never let your sanity bottom out while in the middle of a loop.

## The "Waffle Army" Strategy: Is It Practical?

A popular theory circulating in the community involves the use of the Waffle item to provide a permanent speed boost. The Waffle item creates a consumable on a 30-second cooldown that grants a temporary haste effect. In theory, if two or three players play as Waffle and constantly feed their speed buffs to a single **toons universe distractor** (like Fantasia), that distractor could theoretically outrun even an Infected Copper indefinitely.

While this sounds powerful, the practical application often falls short for several reasons:
1. **Resource Drain:** Dedicating half the team to supporting one distractor significantly slows down machine completion.
2. **Sanity Mismatch:** The supporters (Waffles) will have higher sanity than the person being chased. Eventually, the infected's AI will likely target the supporters instead of the intended distractor.
3. **Map Hazards:** Squiddles and other environmental traps can ruin pathing, making long-term looping nearly impossible regardless of speed.

## Managing Your Sanity and Stamina

To be a successful **toons universe distractor**, you must master the art of the "Socks Reset." Socks are a consumable item with two charges, each capable of restoring 20% of your sanity (for a total of 40% per item). Using these during a chase is vital because it effectively "resets" your agro priority and ensures your stamina regeneration doesn't hit the zero-cap.

### Steps to Perform a Successful Agro Reset:
1. **Lure the Infected:** Pull the target to a safe looping area away from active machines.
2. **Monitor the Bar:** Watch your sanity. When it hits roughly 30%, prepare your Socks.
3. **Use Socks:** Consume a charge to bump your sanity back up. This prevents the infected from switching to your teammates.
4. **Disengage:** Once your stamina is low, lead the infected into a room with multiple exits, break line of sight, and hide to allow your sanity to naturally recover.

> **💡 Tip:** If you see a teammate working on a machine, do not run the infected near them. Because their sanity is likely higher than yours, the infected will almost always "grief" the machine player by switching targets instantly.

## Advanced Distracting: Dealing with Squiddle Floors

Squiddles are the natural enemy of the **toons universe distractor**. These entities mess up pathing and can block off common looping routes. On floors with Squiddles, traditional "infinite loops" become death traps. In 2026, the best distractors use a "Room-to-Room" strategy rather than a "Circular Loop" strategy. This involves moving through the map in a linear fashion, using doors and corners to break line of sight, rather than staying in one large area where Squiddles can accumulate.

## FAQ

**Q: Can any agent be a toons universe distractor?**

A: Technically, yes, but it is highly discouraged. Characters with low base speed cannot outrun infected like Copper or higher-tier threats. It is best to use Fantasia or Fizzle if you intend to take on the primary kiting role for your team.

**Q: Why does the infected ignore me and attack my friend on a machine?**

A: This is due to the Sanity mechanic. In Toons Universe, infected prefer players with higher sanity. If you have been chased for a long time, your sanity is low, making you "less attractive" to the AI than a teammate who has been standing still with a full sanity bar.

**Q: How do I stop my stamina from draining so fast?**

A: Ensure your sanity never hits zero. Once your sanity bar is empty, your stamina regeneration stops, and your current stamina will drain rapidly. Use Socks or break the chase to let your sanity recover before it reaches the critical threshold.

**Q: Is the Waffle strategy worth it in a public lobby?**

A: Usually not. The Waffle strategy requires high coordination between 3-4 players. In public lobbies, it is much more efficient to have one player distract normally while the other three focus entirely on completing the floor objectives as fast as possible.
