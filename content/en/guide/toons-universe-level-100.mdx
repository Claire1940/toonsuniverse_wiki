<Section icon={Swords} title="The Chase Mechanic: Incremental Rewards">

For those playing Distractor roles like Fantasia or Fizzle, the chase mechanic is the single most lucrative source of experience. Unlike other games where distracting is often a thankless job, Toons Universe rewards players for the time they spend keeping the threat occupied. However, these rewards are not flat; they function in specific time increments that peak at 90 seconds.

| Chase Duration | XP Increment | Cumulative XP |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First 15 Seconds** | 15 XP | 15 XP |
| **Following 15 Seconds** | 20 XP | 35 XP |
| **Next 30 Seconds** | 25 XP | 60 XP |
| **Final 30 Seconds** | 30 XP | 90 XP |

Once you reach the 90-second mark in a single chase, you have maximized the XP gain for that specific engagement. At this point, it is often more efficient to disengage, reset, and focus on other objectives like machines to round out your floor score.

<Callout type="warning" title="Diminishing Returns">
Staying in a chase beyond 90 seconds does not provide further incremental XP bonuses. If your goal is reaching **Toons Universe level 100** quickly, break the chase after 90 seconds to find other XP sources.
</Callout>

</Section>
