## Master the Sanity and Stealth Mechanics

One of the most innovative features available at the **toons universe release date** is the Sanity system. In many survival horror games, stealth is a static stat. In Toons Universe, sanity acts as your stealth meter. The more "sane" your character is, the more likely the Infected are to notice you. 

When you are chased by an Infected, your sanity meter depletes over time. Once it hits zero, your stamina will drain rapidly and won't regenerate until the chase ends and your sanity is restored. However, there is a strategic trade-off: Infected prefer targets with higher sanity. This means that a "distractor" with low sanity is actually less appealing to the monsters than a teammate with high sanity who is simply standing nearby.

| Mechanic | Effect on Gameplay | Strategy Tip |
|---|---|---|
| **High Sanity** | High visibility to Infected; full stamina regeneration. | Stay away from active chases to avoid "stealing" aggro. |
| **Low Sanity** | Low visibility (Stealth); stamina drain at zero. | Use this to lose the Infected, but manage your stamina carefully. |
| **Distracting** | Intentionally drawing aggro to allow others to work. | Only attempt this with high-speed agents like **Fantasia** or **Fizzle**. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never run past a teammate working on a machine while you are being chased. Because they likely have higher sanity than you, the Infected will often switch targets to them immediately, effectively "griefing" your teammate.

## Starting Agents: Nimbus vs. Waffle

Upon the **toons universe release date**, all players start with two agents unlocked: Nimbus and Waffle. This is a departure from the "choose one" starter model seen in other games, allowing players to experiment with different playstyles immediately.

Nimbus is widely considered the stronger choice for solo players. His primary ability allows him to restore his stamina to full instantly, though it leaves him vulnerable for a few seconds. In a game where stamina is tied directly to the sanity/chase mechanic, having an on-demand refill is invaluable for surviving long floors alone.

### Waffle: The Co-op Support
Waffle shines in a team environment. She can throw waffles to her teammates, which automatically enter their inventory and provide a speed boost when consumed. While her passive speed boost upon picking up items is somewhat situational, her ability to buff the entire team makes her a top-tier pick for coordinated groups.

## Navigating Event Floors and Machine Types

Progressing through Toons Universe requires completing machines on every floor. There are two primary interaction types: **Hacking** and **Extraction**. Your character's stats determine how quickly these tasks are completed. Once all standard machines are finished, a central "Tower" activates, requiring a few final interactions to power the elevator.

However, the game frequently throws "Event Floors" at players to disrupt their progress. These are random occurrences that can happen on any floor regardless of the season.

*   **Gas Floors:** These floors cause your stamina to drain faster over time. Speed is essential here; you must finish the machines and reach the elevator before your stamina management becomes impossible.
*   **Blackouts:** The lights go out, plunging the floor into darkness. You must locate the breaker box to restore power. You can often spot the breaker in advance by looking for white particle effects.
*   **Elevator Breakdowns:** Usually occurring just as you finish the last machine, the elevator will lose power. You must scour the floor for fuses and return them to the elevator's fuse box to escape.

## Sharing Items and Team Strategy

Success in Toons Universe is heavily dependent on resource management. Items are common, but high-tier heals like Medkits are rare. Occasionally, a vendor named Checkov will appear in the elevator to sell items. It is important to remember that his stock is limited and usually contains fewer items than there are players in the lobby.

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize giving heals to the player with the lowest health or the designated distractor. Buying all the meds for yourself in a full lobby is a quick way to ensure the team fails on later floors.
