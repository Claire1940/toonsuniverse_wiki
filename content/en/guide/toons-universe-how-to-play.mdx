## Understanding Agent Stats and Abilities

Every character in Toons Universe, referred to as an "Agent," comes with a specific set of attributes that determine their effectiveness in various tasks. Choosing the right agent for your playstyle is the first step in learning **toons universe how to play** effectively.

| Stat | Description | Impact on Gameplay |
|---|---|---|
| **Hacking** | Speed at which an agent completes computer objectives. | Faster objective completion on tech-heavy floors. |
| **Extracting** | Speed at which an agent completes machine objectives. | Essential for clearing standard floors quickly. |
| **Sanity** | Determines how slowly the sanity/fear bar drains. | High sanity allows for longer stays in dark or gas-filled areas. |
| **Speed** | The base movement velocity of the agent. | Crucial for outrunning hostile entities. |
| **Stamina** | The amount of energy available for sprinting. | Determines how long you can maintain a chase. |

For beginners, agents like **Waffle** are highly recommended. Waffle provides a supportive role by granting a 30% speed boost and stamina regeneration to targeted allies. Additionally, Waffle gains a temporary 20% speed boost for 10 seconds whenever they pick up an item from the floor, making them an excellent choice for runners and item collectors.

## Toons Universe How to Play: Core Mechanics

The primary goal in Toons Universe is to navigate through floors of a biotech facility, completing a set number of machines or computers to power the elevator to the next level. This loop continues as the difficulty scales and new threats emerge.

### 1. Completing Objectives
Objectives come in two main forms: **Extractors** and **Computers**. While they look different, both require you to stand near them and interact. During the process, you will encounter "skill checks"—timed zones where you must press the spacebar. 

> **💡 Tip:** Some skill checks move significantly faster than others. Pay close attention to the dial speed, as missing a check can alert nearby monsters to your location.

### 2. Managing the Fear Bar
On the left side of your HUD, you will notice a purple bar representing your Fear or Sanity. As you spend time in the facility, especially in hazardous conditions like gas-filled floors, this bar will fill.

*   **Low Fear:** Your agent moves and acts normally.
*   **High Fear:** Your vision becomes hazy, and your stamina begins to drain rapidly.
*   **Zero Sanity:** If the bar fills completely, you will find it nearly impossible to sprint, making you an easy target for hunters.

### 3. Collecting Items and Fuses
Throughout the map, you will find gears, capsules, and other scraps. Collecting these is vital for progression and upgrading your agents. On certain floors, the elevator may malfunction, requiring you to find **Fuses** scattered across the map. You must bring these to the fuse box located near the elevator to proceed.

## Surviving the Entities of Innova

The monsters in Toons Universe are diverse, ranging from slow-moving giants to incredibly fast predators. Understanding their behavior is key to your survival.

| Entity Type | Behavior | Counter-Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Dragon** | Moves slowly at first but gains massive speed upon sight. | Break line of sight using corners and rooms. |
| **Snake** | Often hides or jumps out to surprise players. | Keep your camera moving to check corners frequently. |
| **Bear** | A heavy hitter that relies on sound and proximity. | Avoid running directly into its path; use distractions. |
| **Shadow Clone** | Terrifying versions of the agents themselves. | These often have unique abilities similar to the players. |

When a monster spots you, the music will shift, and your character may show signs of panic. Use your environment to your advantage. Most entities struggle with tight pathfinding around complex furniture or wall corners. If you are playing as a high-speed agent, your role is to "distract" the entity while your teammates with high Hacking or Extracting stats finish the machines.

## Advanced Strategies for Floor 10 and Beyond

As you reach higher floors, the game introduces environmental modifiers. One of the most common is the **Gas Floor**. This modifier causes your sanity to drain constantly regardless of where you are on the map.

*   **Prioritize Speed:** On gas floors, do not linger. Complete machines as a group to finish them faster.
*   **Sanity Items:** Keep an eye out for items that restore sanity or health. Use them only when your bar is in the "danger zone" (the final 25%).
*   **Voice Acting Cues:** Listen to the voice lines of the entities. Some monsters give away their position or their current state (searching vs. chasing) through specific audio cues.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to "loop" monsters in open areas. Their speed often scales the longer a chase lasts, eventually outpacing even the fastest agents.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the difference between Hacking and Extracting in Toons Universe?**

A: Hacking refers specifically to the speed at which you complete computer terminals, while Extracting refers to the speed of completing mechanical machines. Different agents excel at different tasks, so it is best to have a balanced team.

**Q: How do I unlock new agents like Fantasia or Copper?**

A: New agents are typically unlocked using the resources (gears and capsules) you collect during your runs. Some premium or special agents may require specific achievements or event participation.

**Q: Why is my stamina not regenerating?**

A: If your fear bar (the purple bar) is too high, your stamina regeneration is severely penalized. You need to find a safe area or use a sanity-restoring item to lower your fear levels.

**Q: Is Toons Universe playable solo?**

A: While you can play solo, the game is designed for teamwork. Having multiple players allows for roles like "Distractor" and "Objective Specialist," which makes surviving higher floors much easier. Understanding **toons universe how to play** in a group setting is the most effective way to reach the endgame content.
