## The Core XP Mechanics of Toons Universe

To excel as a solo player, you must first understand the granular details of how the game rewards your actions. Every interaction on a floor has a specific XP value attached to it. In a group setting, these opportunities are often split among three or four players, leading to "starvation" of resources. When playing solo, every machine, every fuse, and every second of a chase belongs entirely to you.

The following table outlines the current XP rewards for common actions as of the 2026 season:

| Action | XP Reward | Frequency/Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ability Activation** | 10 XP | First 3 uses per floor |
| **Standard Machine** | 10 XP | Per machine completed |
| **Main Machine** | 20 XP | Per main machine completed |
| **Fixing Blackouts** | 15 XP | Per event occurrence |
| **Collecting Fuses** | 15 XP | Per fuse during breakdowns |
| **Healing Players** | 20 XP | Per heal (Gifter only) |

> **💡 Tip:** Even if you don't need to use your ability for survival, activate it three times as soon as you spawn to bank an easy 30 XP.

## Why Solo Play is the Meta for Leveling

The primary reason to utilize a **toons universe solo guide** strategy is the lack of competition. In a standard lobby, you might only get to complete one or two machines before your teammates finish the rest. In solo mode, you are responsible for every single objective. This effectively triples or quadruples your machine-based XP per run.

Furthermore, solo play allows you to control the flow of the game. You can intentionally prolong a floor to maximize "Chase XP," which is the most lucrative source of experience in the game. In a group, someone might trigger the elevator prematurely, cutting off your ability to farm the monster for points.

### The Chase XP Increment System

Distracting the monster isn't just about survival; it is the fastest way to level up. The developers have implemented a tiered system that rewards players for staying in a chase. The longer you keep the monster's attention, the more XP you earn in 15-to-30-second increments.

| Time Spent in Chase | XP Reward | Cumulative XP |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First 15 Seconds** | 15 XP | 15 XP |
| **Next 15 Seconds** | 20 XP | 35 XP |
| **Next 30 Seconds** | 25 XP | 60 XP |
| **Final 30 Seconds** | 30 XP | **90 XP** |

Once you hit the 90-second mark, you have reached the "soft cap" for that specific chase. To be efficient, you should aim to disengage after 90 seconds, reset the monster's aggro, and focus on finishing the machines.

## Character Strategies for Solo Runs

Not all characters are created equal when it comes to solo play. Your strategy should shift based on whether your character is classified as a Distractor or an Extractor.

### Distractors (Fantasia, Fizzle, etc.)
If you are playing a high-mobility character, your priority is the 90-second chase. 
1. Spawn in and immediately use your ability three times.
2. Locate the monster and initiate a chase.
3. Kite the monster for a full 90 seconds to secure the 90 XP.
4. Use your mobility to lose the monster, then complete the machines to finish the floor.

### Extractors and Others
For slower characters or those focused on objectives, the chase is a secondary bonus.
1. Focus on completing all standard machines immediately.
2. Complete the main machines for the 20 XP bonus.
3. If the monster finds you, try to maintain the chase for at least 30 seconds to snag the first two tiers of rewards (35 XP).
4. Prioritize "Event XP" like blackouts and fuse collecting, as these are often easier for Extractors to handle safely.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not overextend for chase XP if you are playing a character with low stamina. Dying resets your floor progress and can negate the time spent farming.

## The Ultimate Solo Checklist

To ensure you are getting the most out of this **toons universe solo guide**, follow this checklist on every single floor. If you miss one of these steps, you are leaving XP on the table.

1. **The Triple Tap:** Use your character's unique ability 3 times immediately.
2. **Machine Sweep:** Locate and finish every small machine on the map.
3. **The 90-Second Dance:** Engage the monster and keep the chase active for 90 seconds. 
4. **Event Mastery:** If a blackout occurs or the elevator breaks, drop everything to fix it. These are "free" XP drops.
5. **Main Objective:** Complete the main machine only after all other tasks are finished.
6. **The Exit:** Head to the elevator. If you have extra health and the monster is nearby, consider a quick 15-second chase before stepping inside for a final 15 XP boost.

## FAQ

**Q: Is it harder to survive solo in Toons Universe?**

A: Yes, because the monster is always focused on you. However, because you are the only target, the monster's movement becomes more predictable. Without teammates making noise or failing skill checks elsewhere, you can control the monster's positioning more easily.

**Q: Does playing solo reduce the amount of Malachor I earn?**

A: No. In fact, because you are completing more objectives (machines, events) personally, your Malachor gain per match is typically higher than in a group where those rewards are spread out.

**Q: What is the fastest character to level using this toons universe solo guide?**

A: Fantasia and Fizzle are currently the fastest. Their ability to safely maintain 90-second chases allows them to bank the maximum amount of Chase XP on every floor, which is the largest single source of experience available.

**Q: Can I still get the "Healing" XP while playing solo?**

A: Unfortunately, no. The 20 XP for healing is only available in multiplayer. However, the XP you gain from completing all the machines yourself more than makes up for the loss of healing rewards.

**Q: Should I prioritize machines or chases?**

A: You should prioritize machines for consistent progress, but never skip a chase if you have the stamina. A perfect solo run includes all machines AND a 90-second chase. If you have to choose, machines are safer, but chases are faster for pure XP.
