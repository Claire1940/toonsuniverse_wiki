## Understanding the XP Reward System

To level up efficiently, you must first understand where your experience points come from. The game rewards players for a variety of activities, ranging from objective completion to direct interaction with the "toons" that haunt the floors. Unlike many other extraction-style games where only escaping matters, this game rewards the journey as much as the destination.

The following table breaks down the standard XP rewards for common actions performed during a typical match:

| Action | XP Reward | Frequency/Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ability Activation** | 10 XP | First 3 uses per floor |
| **Standard Machine** | 10 XP | Per machine completed |
| **Main Machine** | 20 XP | Per floor completion |
| **Fixing Blackout** | 15 XP | Per event occurrence |
| **Collecting Fuses** | 15 XP | Per fuse during breakdown |
| **Healing Players** | 20 XP | Per successful heal |

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize using your character's unique ability at least three times as soon as you enter a floor. This is an easy 30 XP that many beginners leave on the table.

## The Art of the Chase

One of the most unique aspects of the progression system is how it rewards "Distractors." While many players prefer to stay hidden, the game explicitly incentivizes getting into chases. This is the developers' way of rewarding players who take risks to keep the entities away from those working on machines.

XP for chases functions in 15-to-30-second increments. To get the maximum benefit, you should aim for a chase lasting at least 90 seconds. After 90 seconds, the rewards plateau, meaning you should attempt to lose the entity and return to objectives.

| Chase Duration | Incremental XP | Total Accumulated XP |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0 - 15 Seconds** | +15 XP | 15 XP |
| **15 - 30 Seconds** | +20 XP | 35 XP |
| **30 - 60 Seconds** | +25 XP | 60 XP |
| **60 - 90 Seconds** | +30 XP | **90 XP** |

If you are playing a high-mobility character like Fantasia or Fizzle, the chase is your primary source of experience. Mastering the movement mechanics to maintain a 90-second chase can yield more XP than completing several machines combined.

## Character Roles and Leveling Strategies

Not every character is built for the same task. In this **toons universe beginner guide**, we categorize characters into two primary roles: Extractors and Distractors. Your leveling strategy should shift significantly based on which role your chosen character fits into.

### The Extractor Strategy
Extractors focus on completing machines and handling floor events. If you are playing a character with average or low speed, your goal is to be as efficient as possible with objectives.
*   **Prioritize Machines:** Complete every standard machine you find before touching the main machine.
*   **Event Response:** Be the first to respond to blackouts or elevator breakdowns. The 15 XP per fuse is a massive boost for characters who aren't built for long chases.
*   **Ability Usage:** Use your ability immediately to get your 30 XP "base" for the floor.

### The Distractor Strategy
Distractors like Fantasia or Fizzle thrive on attention.
*   **Engage Early:** Find the entity and initiate a chase.
*   **Time Management:** Use a stopwatch or internal clock to track your 90 seconds. Once you hit the limit, use your abilities to escape.
*   **End-Game Cleanup:** After your chase is done, help finish the main machine to secure the final 20 XP for the floor.

## Why Solo Play is Better for Grinding

While Toons Universe is a social experience, players looking for the fastest possible leveling should consider playing solo. When you are in a group, you are competing with teammates for limited resources. In a solo lobby, every machine, every fuse, and every second of chase time belongs to you.

| Feature | Solo Play | Group Play |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Machine Availability** | High (All machines are yours) | Low (Shared with others) |
| **Chase Consistency** | High (Entity always targets you) | Random (Entity may target others) |
| **Event Rewards** | Maximum (You handle all fuses/fixes) | Split (Teammates may take objectives) |
| **XP Potential** | **Highest per match** | Moderate to Low |

Playing solo allows you to follow a strict checklist: use abilities three times, complete all small machines, fix any blackouts that occur, engage in a 90-second chase, and then finish the main machine. This loop ensures you hit the XP cap for almost every category in a single run.

## Emblems, Charms, and the Road to Level 100

The ultimate goal for most players is reaching level 100 to unlock Character Emblems. These items are not just cosmetic; they often provide significant buffs to a character's primary abilities, making them essential for high-level play.

> **Warning:** Currently, the grind to level 100 is significant. Focus on one character at a time rather than spreading your XP across the entire roster if you want to see the benefits of emblems quickly.

In addition to emblems, leveling up rewards you with Malachor, the premium currency used to purchase charms and trinkets. These items can be equipped to further customize your playstyle, such as increasing your movement speed or reducing the cooldown of your abilities.

For more information on the latest updates and community events, be sure to check the [official Roblox game page](https://www.roblox.com/games/) regularly to stay ahead of the meta.

## Advanced Tips for Efficient Leveling

1.  **Elevator Breakdowns:** During these events, don't just grab one fuse and wait. Try to find and deposit as many as possible. At 15 XP per fuse, a single breakdown event can be more lucrative than completing the main machine.
2.  **Healing Synergy:** If you are playing with a friend, intentionally taking small amounts of damage and healing each other can be a way to farm the 20 XP healing bonus, though this is less efficient than completing objectives.
3.  **The 90-Second Rule:** Never stay in a chase longer than 90 seconds if your only goal is XP. The risk of being caught increases significantly, and you receive 0 additional XP for any time spent in a chase beyond that 90-second mark.
4.  **Main Machine Timing:** Do not finish the main machine until you have exhausted your ability uses and checked the floor for any remaining standard machines. Once the main machine is done, the floor ends, and any missed XP is lost forever.
