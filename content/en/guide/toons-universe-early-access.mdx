## Getting Started: The Core Gameplay Loop

The primary objective in Toons Universe is to navigate through various floors, completing tasks to power up the main elevator and escape. Unlike many "bare-bones" releases in the genre, this game launched with a significant amount of content, featuring multiple Agents with unique voice lines and distinct gameplay roles.

The gameplay is divided into three main phases:
1.  **Task Completion:** Players must locate and interact with levers and computers to extract Malachor, a mysterious green substance.
2.  **Main Machine Activation:** Once standard tasks are finished, the team must converge on a giant Malachor machine to finalize the floor's progress.
3.  **Elevator Extraction:** Players must reach the elevator, which may occasionally malfunction, requiring "Event" style fixes like finding fuses.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on the ceiling. Unlike some games where ceiling hazards merely grab you, the creatures in Toons Universe deal immediate damage if you walk directly beneath them.

## Understanding the Infected and Sanity Mechanics

The enemies in Toons Universe, known as the Infected, are more than just mindless pursuers. Each has a unique design and behavioral gimmick. For instance, **Copper** acts as a literal apex predator with heavy sound design that signals his approach, while **Koi** features a striking visual design that many players consider the best in the game.

### The Sanity Meter
A crucial addition to the **toons universe early access** gameplay is the Sanity meter (the purple bar). This mechanic is designed to prevent players from "looping" Infected indefinitely and making the game too easy for the rest of the team.

*   **Degradation:** The longer an Infected chases you, the more your Sanity bar depletes.
*   **Visual Effects:** As Sanity drops, your vision will begin to blur, making it harder to navigate the map.
*   **Stamina Penalty:** Once your Sanity is low, your stamina will stop regenerating. This effectively puts a timer on how long you can survive a single chase.

| Infected Name | Threat Level | Primary Gimmick |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Copper** | High | High speed and aggressive sound cues. |
| **Koi** | Medium | Rapid spawns and unpredictable pathing. |
| **Grizzly** | Medium | Can "sleep" on the job but wakes up when objectives are completed. |

| Item | Use Count | Effect |

> **⚠️ Warning:** The USB Drive is specifically for computer hacking. Do not waste it on lever objectives (extraction), as it may not provide the same 50% progress boost.

## Advanced Objective Strategies

Success in **toons universe early access** requires more than just running; you must master the skill check system. When hacking or extracting, a circular meter will appear with a blue segment.

1.  **Hit the Blue:** Landing your cursor on the blue segment provides a progress bonus and builds a combo.
2.  **Maintain the Combo:** Successful skill checks in a row will speed up the objective significantly.
3.  **Coordinate the Main Machine:** The final Malachor machine has a radius. If an Agent with a boosting ability (like the DJ) is nearby, the entire team benefits from increased speed.

### Elevator Events
The elevator is not always a safe haven. Occasionally, you will encounter "Elevator Events" where the lift breaks down upon arrival.
*   **Fuse Search:** You must scour the nearby area for fuses and bring them back to the fuse box.
*   **Environmental Hazards:** During these events, you may encounter gas leaks (smoke) or total blackouts, forcing you to rely on sound and teammate callouts.

## FAQ

**Q: How does the Sanity meter work in Toons Universe early access?**

A: The Sanity meter is a purple bar that depletes while you are being chased. As it lowers, your vision blurs and your stamina stops regenerating. This mechanic prevents players from distracting the Infected for too long, forcing a rotation of players to handle the threat.

**Q: Can I use items more than once?**

A: It depends on the item. Consumables like Mints and Coffee are single-use, but equipment like Gloves and Socks typically have two charges before they are consumed.

**Q: What is the best way to speed up hacking objectives?**

A: The most effective way is to use a **USB Drive** to instantly clear 50% of the progress, followed by a **Mint** to increase your manual hacking speed. Additionally, landing "Blue" skill checks consistently will build a combo that further accelerates the process.

**Q: What happens if the elevator breaks down?**

A: If the elevator breaks, you must find fuses scattered around the floor and bring them back to the fuse box. Be careful, as the Infected will still be active, and you may have to deal with blackouts or gas leaks during this phase.
